## History and Significance

Friedrich Schiller, born in 1759, was a central figure in the German literary movement known as Sturm und Drang, which emphasized emotional expression and the power of individualism. He is best known for his plays such as "Die Räuber" (The Robbers) and "Wilhelm Tell," as well as his philosophical essays and poetry. Schiller's work often explores themes of freedom, morality, and the human condition, making him a key influencer in the world of literature and thought.

The Schiller-Denkmal was erected to commemorate Schiller's legacy and his contributions to the arts and philosophy.
