## History and Significance

Friedrich Schiller was born in 1759 and became one of the foremost figures of the German literary scene, known for his impactful plays, poems, and philosophical writings. His works often grappled with themes of freedom, beauty, and the human condition, and he played a pivotal role in shaping German romanticism and the Sturm und Drang movement. Schiller's relationship with Johann Wolfgang von Goethe, another literary giant, further solidified his place in the literary canon, as their correspondence and collaboration influenced the direction of German literature.

The Schillerhaus serves not only as a museum dedicated to Schiller's life but also as a reflection of the cultural milieu of the time.
