The Jesuitenkirche was constructed during the period when the Jesuit order was actively engaged in educational and missionary work in Heidelberg and beyond. While specific dates of its establishment are not provided, the church is emblematic of the Counter-Reformation's impact on Germany, particularly in regions like Baden-Württemberg where the Jesuits sought to counteract the spread of Protestantism.

The church became a focal point for the community, where various religious and social activities took place, further embedding it into the fabric of Heidelberg's history.
