## Architecture and Features

Marienburg is characterized by its robust medieval architecture, which reflects the defensive needs of its time. The castle is constructed from local stone, giving it an earthy appearance that seamlessly integrates with the surrounding landscape. Its formidable walls, designed to withstand sieges, are punctuated by narrow windows and arrow slits, showcasing the functional aspects of medieval design.

One of the most striking features of Marienburg is its imposing towers, which rise above the castle grounds. These towers not only served as lookout points for potential threats but also enhanced the castle's overall aesthetic, contributing to its commanding presence. The layout of the castle often includes a central courtyard, around which various buildings are arranged, providing a glimpse into the daily life of its inhabitants.

Visitors can explore the remnants of the castle's interior, which may include chambers that once housed nobility, storerooms, and possibly even chapels. The architecture of Marienburg exemplifies the blend of utility and artistry typical of medieval fortifications, where every structural element was designed with purpose in mind.
