## Overview

The Ancient and Primeval Beech Forests of the Carpathians and Other Regions of Europe represent one of the last remaining natural temperate beech forests in the world. This UNESCO World Heritage site spans several countries, including Germany, and serves as a vital sanctuary for biodiversity. The forests, characterized by their impressive age and biological diversity, provide crucial ecosystems that support various flora and fauna. These forests not only serve as important ecological reserves but also play a significant role in climate regulation, highlighting their global importance.

Located in the Subcarpathian Voivodeship, the site covers an extensive area that encompasses pristine beech forests, showcasing the resilience of nature over the centuries. The forests serve as a living laboratory, allowing scientists to study the intricate relationships between various species and their habitats. Visitors can immerse themselves in the tranquil beauty of these ancient woods, experiencing the serenity and majesty that only such an environment can offer.
