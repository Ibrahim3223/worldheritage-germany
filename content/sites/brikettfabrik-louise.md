The production of briquettes, which are compacted blocks of coal dust mixed with a binding agent, became a vital source of fuel, particularly during periods of increasing energy demands. This facility not only contributed to the local economy but also reflected broader industrial trends that shaped the region.

The museum's establishment serves to preserve the memory of this important industry and its impact on both local and wider contexts.
