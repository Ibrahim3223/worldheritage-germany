Augsburg has a storied industrial history, known for its contributions to manufacturing and engineering since the Middle Ages. The museum focuses on turbines, a technology that has played a crucial role in the development of energy systems globally.

Turbines, which convert fluid energy into mechanical energy, have revolutionized power generation and propulsion systems. Their applications range from hydropower plants to jet engines, making them integral to modern infrastructure and transportation. The museum's exhibits delve into the various types of turbines, including steam, gas, and water turbines, and their respective roles in the advancement of technology.

The museum serves as an educational hub for schools and engineering students, offering insights into the engineering challenges and successes of the past.
