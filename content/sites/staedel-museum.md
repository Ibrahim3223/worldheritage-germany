## History and Significance

The Städel Museum has cultivated a rich cultural heritage since its inception. Founded as a private collection by the banker Johann Friedrich Städel, the museum's mission has always been to enhance the public's understanding and appreciation of art. Over the centuries, it has transformed into one of Germany’s premier art institutions, reflecting both the aspirations of its founder and the evolving tastes of the art world.

The museum’s collection is remarkable, encompassing works from the 14th century to contemporary art. This vast array of pieces not only showcases the technical skills of renowned artists but also highlights the cultural contexts from which these works emerged. Each painting, sculpture, and print tells a story, whether it be a reflection of societal changes or a personal narrative of the artist.

Through the years, the Städel Museum has played a pivotal role in the cultural landscape of Frankfurt.
