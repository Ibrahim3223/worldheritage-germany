Originally established in the 19th century, these small plots of land were created as a response to rapid urbanization and industrialization. As cities expanded, many inhabitants found themselves disconnected from nature and the agricultural roots of their communities. Allotments offered a solution, providing urban residents with the opportunity to cultivate their own food, enjoy the outdoors, and foster social connections with their neighbors.

The German Allotment Museum plays a crucial role in preserving this history and highlighting the cultural significance of allotment gardens. These gardens have not only served as sources of sustenance but also as communal spaces where people gather, share knowledge, and celebrate a love for gardening.
