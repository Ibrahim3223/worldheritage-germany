## Architecture and Features

Kröpeliner Tor is an excellent example of medieval fortification architecture, characterized by its robust structure and decorative elements. The gate features two large towers flanking the central archway, which was designed to accommodate the passage of both pedestrians and vehicles. Its imposing presence is accentuated by the use of brick, a material that was commonly employed in Northern German architecture during the Middle Ages.

The design of Kröpeliner Tor incorporates elements that are both functional and aesthetic. The towers are crowned with crenellations, allowing for defensive positioning. Over time, the gate has been preserved and restored, maintaining its historical integrity while adapting to modern needs. As a city gate, Kröpeliner Tor also features small openings that were historically used for defense and surveillance. Today, the gate stands not only as a remnant of the past but also as a focal point for cultural events and gatherings within the city.
