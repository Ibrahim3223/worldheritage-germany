## History and Significance

Hohle Fels has played a crucial role in archaeological studies, particularly concerning the Upper Paleolithic period. Over the centuries, this cave has been a focal point for researchers aiming to understand the lives of our ancient ancestors. The site is most renowned for the discovery of the Venus of Hohle Fels, a small figurine that dates back to approximately 35,000 to 40,000 years ago. This small carving is considered one of the oldest known representations of the human form, highlighting the artistic skills of early humans and their capacity for symbolic thought.

The findings at Hohle Fels indicate that this site was not merely a shelter but a space for social and cultural activities.
