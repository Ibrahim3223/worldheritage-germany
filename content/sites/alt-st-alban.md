## History and Significance

Founded in 1200, Alt St. Alban represents a significant chapter in Cologne's ecclesiastical history. The church’s construction aligns with a broader movement across Europe during this period, where Gothic architecture began to flourish. Characterized by its pointed arches, ribbed vaults, and flying buttresses, the architectural style speaks to the period's innovative spirit.

The design of Alt St. Alban reflects the artistic trends of its time, with features that were meant to inspire awe and draw the faithful closer to the divine.
