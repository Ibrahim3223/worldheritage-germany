## History and Significance

Geusenfriedhof's origins are deeply intertwined with the history of Cologne and the broader socio-political context of Germany. This cemetery serves as a memorial to the Geusen, a group of Dutch Protestants who sought refuge and support in the city during periods of religious persecution. Their presence in Cologne is a testament to the city’s role as a sanctuary for those fleeing oppression, embodying themes of resilience and solidarity.

Throughout its history, Geusenfriedhof has witnessed the intersection of various cultural movements and historical events.
