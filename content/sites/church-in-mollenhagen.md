## Architecture and Features

The Church in Möllenhagen is crafted from brick, a material that contributes to both its durability and aesthetic appeal. This choice of construction material is typical of Gothic Revival architecture, which emphasizes verticality and light, aiming to inspire awe through its grand forms.

Visitors will appreciate the church's distinct architectural style, characterized by pointed arches, ribbed vaults, and intricate detailing. These elements are hallmarks of the Gothic Revival movement, which sought to revive the grandeur of medieval ecclesiastical structures. 

As you explore the church, take note of its noteworthy features, which may include ornamental stonework and stained glass windows that enhance the interior with colorful light. These elements not only serve decorative purposes but also narrate biblical stories and local lore, enriching your understanding of the site.
