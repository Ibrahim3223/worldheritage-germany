## Overview

Fischtor, an evocative remnant of Arnstadt's historical fortifications, serves as a striking reminder of the city’s architectural and cultural heritage. Located in the Ilm-Kreis region of Thuringia, Germany, this former city gate was originally part of the Schlossgartenmauer, the castle garden wall, and has since been repurposed into a residence for the court fisherman. This transformation reflects not only the practical uses of historical structures but also the adaptive nature of urban development over time.

Arnstadt, one of the oldest towns in Thuringia, is known for its rich history, which is intricately tied to the broader narrative of German heritage. The Fischtor is an important element of this narrative, inviting visitors to explore its significance within the context of the city’s fortifications.
