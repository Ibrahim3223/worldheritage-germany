## History and Significance

The Geschwister-Scholl-Haus is named after the Scholl siblings, Hans and Sophie, who were pivotal figures in the German resistance against the Nazi regime through their involvement with the White Rose movement. Though the building itself does not directly relate to their activities, it symbolizes the ideals they stood for—freedom of thought, the importance of education, and the courage to challenge oppressive regimes. Their legacy continues to inspire discussions around ethics in education and the role of intellectual dissent in society.

The school's history is reflective of the broader changes in the German educational system. Over the years, it has adapted to the needs of its students and has been a pivotal center for various educational reforms.
