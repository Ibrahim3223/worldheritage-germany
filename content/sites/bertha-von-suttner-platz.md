## History and Significance

The significance of Bertha-von-Suttner-Platz stems from its namesake, Bertha von Suttner, who is recognized for her pioneering role in the peace movement and her advocacy for disarmament. While specific dates of establishment for the square are not noted, its designation reflects a broader cultural acknowledgment of von Suttner's contributions to peace and her influence on global diplomacy. She is best known for her book "Die Waffen nieder!" (Lay Down Your Arms!), which was one of the first works to advocate for peace and the abolition of war, earning her the Nobel Peace Prize in 1905.

The square serves as a reminder of the importance of dialogue and peace in society. In a modern context, it provides a platform for discussions around current social issues, including activism related to peace and sustainability. As a public square, it plays a role in fostering community engagement, allowing citizens to gather, celebrate, and express their views.
