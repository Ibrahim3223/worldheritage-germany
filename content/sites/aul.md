With its strategic position, it offers insight into the historical significance of the town, which dates back to medieval times. The Aul not only serves as a reminder of Zwingenberg's fortified heritage but also adds a unique character to the area, making it an intriguing stop for history enthusiasts and casual visitors alike. ## Architecture and Features

The Aul stands as a robust structure, characterized by its utilitarian design intended for fortification. As part of the city wall, it showcases the architectural elements typical of medieval military buildings. The tower is constructed from local stone, ensuring both strength and durability. Its thick walls were designed to withstand attacks, while narrow openings provide strategic visibility for defenders.

Distinct features of the Aul include arrow slits, which were essential for archers during sieges, allowing them to defend the tower while minimizing exposure. The overall shape of the tower is practical, contributing to its defensive capabilities while also enhancing its imposing appearance. 

Visitors to the site will appreciate the craftsmanship and thought that went into its construction. While specific ornamental details may be limited, the Aul’s solid form speaks volumes about the era's architectural priorities—functionality and protection over decoration.
