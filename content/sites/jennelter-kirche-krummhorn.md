The Jennelter Kirche is an impressive structure that exemplifies Romanesque architecture. This style is characterized by its robust and solid appearance, featuring rounded arches, thick walls, and minimal ornamentation. The use of brick as the primary material not only contributes to the church's durability but also adds to its visual appeal.

Visitors will appreciate the simplicity and strength of the church’s design. Key features include the harmonious proportions of its exterior and the sturdy construction that has allowed it to withstand the test of time. The layout of the church typically reflects the traditional arrangement found in many Romanesque buildings, which often includes a nave flanked by aisles and a prominent apse at one end. This design creates a welcoming and communal atmosphere for worshippers and visitors alike.
