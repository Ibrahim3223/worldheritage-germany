He was not merely a composer; he was also an accomplished performer and an influential figure in the development of various musical forms. The Telemann-Museum serves as a homage to his extensive output, which includes over 3,000 compositions across a wide range of genres, including orchestral, chamber, and vocal music.

Telemann's career flourished during a time when Hamburg was a hotbed of musical innovation. He held various important musical positions throughout his life, including that of the city’s music director. His ability to blend different musical styles, incorporating folk elements and influences from other cultures, set him apart from his contemporaries. The museum highlights these aspects of his work, as well as his role in the evolution of the German school of composition.

The establishment of the Telemann-Museum is significant for both cultural and educational purposes.
