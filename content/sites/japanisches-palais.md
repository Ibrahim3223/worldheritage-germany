## History and Significance

The palace was constructed as a part of Dresden's extensive royal palaces and gardens, which were developed to showcase the power and taste of the Saxon electors. The Japanisches Palais stands as a cultural landmark, highlighting the legacy of the Enlightenment period where knowledge and artistic expression flourished. This period saw an increased curiosity about the cultures of Asia, leading to the establishment of collections, exhibitions, and even diplomatic missions aimed at fostering relationships with the East.
