## History and Significance

The Fugger and Welser families were influential in shaping Augsburg's identity during the late Middle Ages and the Renaissance. The Fugger family, founded by Jakob Fugger in the late 15th century, became one of the most powerful banking families in Europe. Their wealth allowed them to finance kings and emperors, and they played a crucial role in the development of the European banking system. The Welser family, contemporaries of the Fuggers, also made their mark as powerful merchants and financiers. They were known for their explorations and colonial ventures, including significant participation in trade with the Americas.

The museum not only honors the achievements of these dynasties but also reflects on the broader socio-economic changes of the time.
