## Architecture and Features

The Japanischer Garten features a delicate blend of natural and architectural elements that exemplify traditional Japanese garden design. Upon entering the garden, visitors are greeted by a peaceful setting characterized by winding paths, ornamental plants, and tranquil water features. 

Notable features include carefully curated landscapes that often include koi ponds, stone lanterns, and meticulously pruned trees. These elements are not merely decorative; they each carry symbolic meanings, from the koi that represent perseverance to the lanterns that guide visitors along the paths. 

The garden's layout emphasizes asymmetry, a core principle of Japanese design, which creates a sense of natural beauty and balance. Various plant species, including flowering cherry trees and bamboo, add seasonal color and texture, enhancing the garden's appeal throughout the year. 

Visitors may also find traditional structures such as tea houses or pagodas, which serve as focal points within the garden. These structures often provide spaces for reflection or gatherings, further enriching the visitor experience.
