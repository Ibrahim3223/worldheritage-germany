## Architecture and Features

Mary's Church is a prime example of Brick Gothic architecture, which flourished in Northern Germany during the late Middle Ages. This style is characterized by its use of brick as the primary building material, showcasing the craftsmanship and ingenuity of its builders. The church's exterior presents a rugged yet elegant appearance, with detailed brickwork that tells a story of its own.

As you explore the church, pay attention to the unique architectural elements that define its character.
