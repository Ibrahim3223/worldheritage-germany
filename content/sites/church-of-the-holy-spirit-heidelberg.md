## Architecture and Features

The Church of the Holy Spirit exemplifies Gothic architecture, characterized by its pointed arches, ribbed vaults, and flying buttresses. These elements are not only aesthetically pleasing but also structurally significant, allowing for taller ceilings and larger windows that invite light into the space.

Visitors should take note of the intricate details found throughout the church. The façade showcases elaborate stone carvings that depict biblical scenes and figures, inviting closer inspection. Inside, the soaring ceilings create an atmosphere of grandeur, while the stained glass windows add color and depth to the interior, illuminating the sacred space with ethereal light.

The choice of materials used in its construction reflects both functionality and artistry typical of Gothic design.
