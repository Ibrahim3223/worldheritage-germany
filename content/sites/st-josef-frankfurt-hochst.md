Founded in 1908, St. Josef was designed by architect Richard Saran. While the church has primarily served its local community over the years, it stands as an important representation of the Romanesque Revival movement in Germany. This architectural style often draws inspiration from medieval forms, characterized by robust structures and intricate detailing.

The construction of St. Josef coincided with a period of growth and development in Frankfurt, as the city expanded to accommodate an increasing population. This church became a cornerstone for the community, symbolizing both faith and resilience.
